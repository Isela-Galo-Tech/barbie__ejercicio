History

Barbie: The story of the world's most popular doll.

On March 9, 1959, the American company Mattel officially put the Barbie doll on sale, without imagining that it would mark future generations and become fashionable. This doll, which today turns 64 years old, has undergone many changes in its history up to the present day, although it has always been faithful to the avant-garde. HISTORY OF BARBIE The creation of Barbie is credited to the company's CEO, Ruth Handler. In the mid-1950s, her daughter preferred to play with dolls with adult features rather than the children's dolls that were more common and popular at the time. Therefore, together with her husband, businessman Harold Matson, Handler created Barbie, based on the inspiration of the German doll Bild Lill, which was sold in the European country from 1955 to 1964. THE FIRST 1959 VERSION She is such a cultural icon that in 1974, a section of New York's Times Square was renamed Barbie Boulevard for a week, and in 1985, artist Andy Warhol created a painting of the blonde doll. Another notable event occurred in 1971, when the Malibu Barbie was the first to face forward, as until then she had a side view. Ten years later, the first black and Latin American dolls appeared. Last year, Mattel launched a new line of gender-neutral dolls for boys, girls and teens. Available in a variety of skin tones, each doll comes with two wigs in different lengths and styles, including straight and wavy, and many options for children to create the doll's look.
